Experience Ethiopia's Ancient Soul

Journey through the cradle of civilization on this 10-day expedition across the Ethiopian Highlands. This curated route takes you from the island monasteries of Lake Tana to the medieval castles of Gondar, culminating in the awe-inspiring monolithic churches of Lalibela—often called the Eighth Wonder of the World.

coffee Local Customs & Traditions

Ethiopian culture is deeply rooted in hospitality. You will participate in the daily **Coffee Ceremony (Buna)**, a multi-hour ritual involving roasting beans and serving three rounds of coffee (Abol, Tona, and Baraka).

  • Respect religious sites by removing shoes and wearing modest attire (white wraps provided).
  • "Gursha" is a gesture of hospitality where people feed one another by hand during meals.
  • Shoulder-shaking dance (Eskista) is common at social gatherings—join in!

Historic Route Itinerary

1-3

Bahir Dar & Lake Tana Monasteries

directions_boat

Boat excursion on Lake Tana to visit 14th-century island monasteries. Witness the Blue Nile Falls and enjoy a traditional sunset coffee ceremony on the lakeshore.

4-6

Gondar: The Camelot of Africa

castle

Explore the Fasil Ghebbi royal enclosure, a UNESCO site of 17th-century castles. Visit the church of Debre Berhan Selassie with its famous ceiling of winged angels.

7-10

Rock-Hewn Churches of Lalibela

church

Immersion into the 11 medieval churches carved out of solid volcanic rock. Participate in a morning mass and a special farewell banquet with traditional dancers.
